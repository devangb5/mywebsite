The Beginning

Dung Tran, owner of Fresh Out the Box and co-owner of The Streatery in Louisville, Kentucky, has built a successful career through passion and perseverance. Originally from Vietnam and moving to the U.S. at four, he began in the beauty industry with a nail salon. However, his love for cooking led him to open a food truck in Louisville, where he created Asian fusion cuisine that blended Vietnamese and Korean flavors. This venture not only showcased his creativity but also helped him connect with the community, laying the groundwork for his thriving culinary career.

The First Brick-and-Mortar: Fresh Out the Box

In 2020, Dung expanded his business by opening Fresh Out the Box, his first brick-and-mortar location at the bustling Logan Street Market. Here, he offers a menu that blends Vietnamese, Chinese, Korean, and Japanese cuisine, featuring popular items like bulgogi bowls, bibimbap, and sesame noodles. The customizable nature of his dishes, particularly the bulgogi bowl, allows customers to personalize their meals, making it a hit among locals.

While transitioning from a food truck to a brick-and-mortar establishment came with challenges, it also provided year-round operation, especially during colder months. However, Dung had to adapt to the increased consistency and rigors of running a full-time restaurant.

The Streatery: A New Chapter in Neighborhood Dining
